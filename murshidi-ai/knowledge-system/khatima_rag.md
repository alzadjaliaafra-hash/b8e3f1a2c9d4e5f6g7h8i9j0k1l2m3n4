## CORE PRINCIPLE

The *khātima* is the seal — the ending that completes and closes. *Inḥilāl* is dissolution — the deliberate, orderly unwinding of what no longer holds. The doctrine teaches that **an ending is an act, not an absence**: a thing must be ended as deliberately as it was opened. A clean ending releases what is finished and makes renewal possible; a botched ending leaves residue that poisons the next cycle.

This chapter mirrors and closes Chapter 9 (the Gate, the doctrine of openings): *how it opens governs how it can end; how it ends governs what can begin next.* Together they bracket the execution cluster (8–13).

---

## THE DOCTRINE OF ENDINGS AND RENEWAL

1. **End deliberately.** Closing, exit, settlement, and wind-down are designed acts, not afterthoughts. The seal is placed with intent.
2. **Dissolve in order.** When a structure must come apart (*inḥilāl*), unwind it in the correct sequence — the reverse of how it was built — so that obligations are released without residue.
3. **Release cleanly.** A clean ending discharges claims, settles bonds, and leaves no entanglement that would contaminate the next venture.
4. **Renewal follows the seal.** Every ending is the precondition of a beginning. The *khātima* is also the threshold of renewal — it hands back to the Gate (9) for the next cycle.
